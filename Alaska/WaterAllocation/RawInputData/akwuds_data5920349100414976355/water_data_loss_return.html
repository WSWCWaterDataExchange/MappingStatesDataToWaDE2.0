Description:
Abstract:
The Alaska Hydrologic Survey (AHS) within the Alaska Department of Natural Resources (ADNR), Division of Mining, Land and Water (DMLW), Water Resources Section, is mandated by Alaska Statute (AS) 41.08.017, AS 41.08.020 and Department Order 115 (Noah, 1994) to collect, record, and require filing of data on the quantity, location, and quality of water in the subsurface, surface, or along the coasts. Additionally, our legislated program elements under AS 41.08 include publishing data on the water of the State, coordinating water-data collection activities by state agencies, and archival of historic and current water use data.

Water use quantities and intake locations are provided by water right/authorization holders. Latitude and longitude coordinates may be entered as decimal degrees, or the mapping tool may be used to pan, zoom, and click on the intake/withdrawal location. Data submitted by users are cross-checked with the file-of-record. In some instances, the intake location proposed in the application contained in the file-of record may differ from the as-constructed intake location. Follow-up discussions with water right/authorization holders are periodically conducted. Additionally, site inspections are occasionally conducted to verify location coordinates for intake/discharge locations, or verify use of meters.

Purpose:
To depict estimated intake/withdrawal locations with a visual degree of accuracy.
